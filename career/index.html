I love to lead

I am a business leader.

I have a BA from Stanford University, and I am pursuing my MBA at the Kellogg School of Management. Kellogg and Stanford?! Yes, I am smart. I’m glad we established that.

Beyond intellectual challenge, there are two things that drive me in my career: Mission and Relationships.

Mission

I care about the mission of my company, the greater vision that we are working toward. I was mission-motivated in my first career out of college, where I worked for a nonprofit called InnVision Shelter Network. We supported homeless families and individuals seeking to regain affordable housing, and we did it effectively and efficiently.

After graduating from Kellogg, I will begin my post-MBA life at LinkedIn, a technology company striving to connect the world's professionals to make them more productive and successful. LinkedIn puts its members first and works to build a network where everyone has access to employment and educational information that will boost their careers and their livelihood. That is a mission I am excited to support.

Relationships

I love people. I probably even like you! Friends and colleagues are what make my career fun and fulfilling. It makes sense that my starting role at LinkedIn will be “Relationship Manager.”

Of all career-oriented relationships, I find mentors to be the most precious. I have been fortunate to come across life-changing mentors in my nonprofit career and at Kellogg. These people see my potential before I do, and guide me with invaluable wisdom. I strive to become a mentor and leader myself, so that I may use my skills and perspective to uncover others’ promising futures.
